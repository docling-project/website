title: Clustering items from adaptively collected inconsistent feedback
authors: S Gupta, PWJ Staar, C de Sainte Marie
date: 2024-05-01
venue: International Conference on Artificial Intelligence and Statistics (AISTATS 2024)
summary: "Studies clustering in a query-based model where a learner repeatedly queries an oracle to determine if two items belong to the same cluster. These queries are costly and the oracle's responses are marred by inconsistency and noise. The learner's goal is to adaptively make a small number of queries and return the correct clusters for all n items with high confidence. Develops efficient algorithms using the sequential hypothesis testing framework with high probability upper bounds on sample complexity. Complements analysis with an information-theoretic lower bound. Shows that the algorithm for two clusters is nearly optimal when the oracle's error probability is a constant, providing theoretical guarantees for practical clustering scenarios with unreliable feedback."
